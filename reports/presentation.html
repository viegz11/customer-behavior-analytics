RFM Analysis

Recency · Frequency · Monetary

RFM scoring turns raw transactions into customer value groups by measuring how recently, how often, and how much customers buy.

R
Recency
How recently a customer purchased.
F
Frequency
How often a customer purchases.
M
Monetary
How much revenue a customer contributes.
RFM Score
Customers were ranked and scored to identify Champions, Loyal Customers, Regular Customers, and At-Risk Customers.

Machine Learning

K-Means clustering revealed behavior groups

Clustering adds a machine learning layer by grouping similar customer profiles based on RFM behavior.

1
Prepare RFM features
Standardize Recency, Frequency, and Monetary variables.
2
Choose cluster count
Use elbow method to identify the ideal number of groups.
3
Fit K-Means
Assign customers into behavior-based clusters.

Cluster Analysis

Business Interpretation
Clustering highlights high-value clusters, dormant customers, and growth segments that can be targeted differently.
High frequency + high monetary = premium customers
Medium engagement = growth opportunity
Low recency = reactivation needed
